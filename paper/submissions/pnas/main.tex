\begin{center}\noindent\fbox{\parbox{0.94\linewidth}{%
\small\textbf{Significance.}\;
Casualty figures in armed conflict are typically reported by sources with strong, opposing political incentives, and aggregated estimates inherit those biases. We show that the civilian-to-combatant death ratio is jointly constrained by demographic and manpower facts measured by independent institutions, so that the truth lies in a narrow identified set even when every belligerent claim is treated as adversarial. We define a contradiction radius---the standardised perturbation of independent inputs needed to rationalise a disputed claim---and apply it to the Gaza war 2023--2026, finding that the public Israel Defense Forces figure of $17$--$25$k combatants killed is roughly $20$ standard errors away from feasibility, while a spatial Bayesian posterior on the same inputs gives $q=2.5\%$ \mbox{$[1.6\%,3.8\%]$}.
}}\end{center}

\paragraph{Materials and Methods.} The Gaza spatial Bayesian posterior is computed by importance sampling over a five-governorate $\times$ 80-cell discretisation, with militant cell density drawn from a clustering prior, strike intensity proportional to a power of the militant share, and civilian adult-male exposure modelled as a multiplier $\mu\in[1,\bar\mu]$ over women-and-children hazard. Posteriors target the joint of total deaths, demographic shares, and Hamas/PIJ pre-war manpower; $200{,}000$ samples, ESS $\approx 2{,}300$. Code, simulator, and inputs are deposited in a public repository (URL upon acceptance).
